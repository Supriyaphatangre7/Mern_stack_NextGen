Ramen


Ramen is a Japanese noodle soup dish consisting of Chinese-style wheat noodles served in a flavorful broth, often topped with ingredients like sliced pork, boiled eggs, nori (seaweed), and green onions.

Regional Variations: Different regions of Japan have their own styles of ramen, such as Sapporo's miso ramen, Hakata's tonkotsu ramen, and Tokyo's shoyu ramen, each showcasing unique flavors and toppings.

Global Popularity: Ramen has become a global culinary favorite, with instant ramen being a convenient and widely consumed version, while traditional ramen shops offer authentic, handcrafted bowls for a more gourmet experience.
