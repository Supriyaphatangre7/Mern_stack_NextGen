Biryani

Biryani is a celebratory rice and meat dish cherished in the Indian sub-continent. A traditional biryani consists of fluffy basmati rice layered over tender & succulent pieces of meat, accompanied with the mesmerizing aromas of spices, herbs & caramelized onions.

Origin The name comes from the Persian word biryani which means "fried" or "roasted". Biryani was first recorded in the Deccan region of India, and may have been brought there by merchants.

Variations There are many regional variations of biryani, including Hyderabadi, Muradabadi, and Sindhi.

Ingredients Biryani is typically made with basmati rice, meat or fish, and spices like saffron and cumin. Other ingredients may include yogurt, ghee, potatoes, onions, and dried fruits.
